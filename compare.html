VoiceApp

VoiceApp is built around a single idea: hold a hotkey, speak, release, and your words type themselves wherever your cursor is. It ships with two private on-device engines — Whisper (accurate, 90+ languages) and Parakeet (very fast, English) — and lets you bring your own API key for cloud models like Grok, OpenAI, and ElevenLabs Scribe when you want maximum accuracy. Because it types as keyboard input, it works identically in every app. Best for people who want control over the model and privacy without fiddling with settings.

MacWhisper

MacWhisper is a well-known Mac app focused on transcribing audio files and recordings with Whisper, and it also offers dictation. It's a strong choice if your main need is transcribing existing audio and video files. For always-available push-to-talk dictation into any app, a purpose-built dictation flow like VoiceApp's tends to feel more direct.

Apple Dictation

Apple's built-in Dictation is free, already on your Mac, and private on-device. It's a great baseline. Its limits: you can't choose or upgrade the speech model, accuracy on technical vocabulary is fixed, and there's no custom-vocabulary or cloud-AI option. If Apple Dictation already does everything you need, you may not need anything else — VoiceApp is for when you want better models and more control.

Which should you pick?

If you mostly transcribe audio files, look at MacWhisper. If Apple Dictation's accuracy is enough for you, it's free and fine. If you want push-to-talk dictation into any app, your choice of on-device or cloud AI models, and custom vocabulary — that's exactly what VoiceApp is built for.
